FAQ

Common Questions

What is an educational psychologist?

An educational psychologist is a doctoral-level professional who specialises in how children and adults learn. We use standardised assessments to identify learning difficulties, cognitive strengths, and the best strategies to support progress in education.

How is this different from what the school can do?

Schools can carry out screening and provide classroom-based support, but they cannot conduct the in-depth cognitive and educational assessments that an educational psychologist provides. My reports carry weight with local authorities and exam boards in ways that school-based assessments typically do not.

How long does the process take?

From initial consultation to receiving your report, most families can expect a turnaround of three to four weeks. The assessment session itself typically lasts a full morning or afternoon.

Do you assess adults?

Yes. I work with university students and adults who suspect they may have specific learning difficulties such as dyslexia. I also carry out DSA needs assessments for students applying for Disabled Students' Allowance.

Where do assessments take place?

I conduct assessments in person at a suitable location in London. For some assessments, I can travel to your child's school. We'll arrange the most appropriate setting during our initial call.

Will the report help with an EHCP application?

Yes. My reports are specifically designed to meet the evidence requirements for Education, Health and Care Plan applications and annual reviews. They include the level of detail that local authorities expect.
