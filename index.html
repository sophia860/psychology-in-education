What Katherine Assesses

Assessments That Make a Difference

Every assessment includes a detailed written report with practical, evidence-based recommendations. Schools and exam boards accept all reports.

Full Educational Assessment

The comprehensive baseline — cognitive ability, memory, literacy, numeracy, attention, and social and emotional factors. Appropriate where you want a complete picture or where EHCP evidence is needed.

EHCP Assessments & Reports

New applications, second opinions, and annual reviews. Reports written in the specific binding language that prevents vague EHCP commitments. Katherine knows what local authority panels need to see.

Dyslexia Assessment

Reading, phonological processing, spelling, and full literacy profile. Follows SASC guidelines and is accepted by all exam boards and universities.

ADHD Assessment

Attention, executive function, working memory, and behavioural profile using validated rating scales, including parent and teacher completion.

DSA Assessments

Meets Student Finance England requirements. Conducted at London and Yorkshire bases. Helps university students access the support they are entitled to.

Access Arrangements

Extra time and exam accommodations for GCSE and A-Level. Meets JCQ requirements so schools can apply with confidence.

Katherine also assesses for dyscalculia, dyspraxia / DCD, and autism & social communication difficulties. Not sure which applies? That’s precisely what an assessment is for.

About Katherine

Katherine Sharkey

Katherine has worked as an educational and child psychologist since 2001 — in private practice and alongside local authorities across London and Yorkshire. Before that, she was a classroom teacher. She holds two master’s degrees, an undergraduate degree in psychology, and a PGCE.

She works alone. That means the psychologist who speaks to you is the same psychologist who assesses your child and writes the report. When she writes a recommendation, she thinks about the teacher who will read it on a Monday morning, at the end of a long day. Will they know exactly where to start? If the answer is no, she rewrites it.

That standard — reports written to be acted on, not filed — is what separates her practice from every other educational psychologist in London.
